Journey Patient Care

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 20, 2026

Journey Patient Care does not collect your information. There is no account to create, no analytics, and no server to send anything to. Everything you record stays encrypted on your own device.

Journey Patient Care (“JPC”, “the app”) is a caregiving app published by TerraVida Organics LLC. This policy explains what the app does with the information you put into it. The short version is above. The rest of this page is the detail behind it.

What we collect

Nothing.

JPC has no user accounts, no sign-in, no analytics or tracking software, no advertising, and no crash reporting service. The app does not create a profile about you, and it does not transmit the information you enter to us or to anyone else.

Where your information lives

Everything you enter into JPC is stored in the app's own storage on your device and encrypted there. That includes medications, appointments, symptoms, vitals, notes, contacts, documents, and any photographs or scanned cards you add.

The encryption key is held in your device's secure hardware keystore and is protected by your device unlock or biometric. It is not extractable, and it is not known to us.

What we can see

Nothing. This is not a promise about our intentions, it is a description of how the app is built. Your records never leave your device, so there is no copy of them for us to access, hand over, or lose.

We have no ability to recover your data on your behalf, and no ability to reset a forgotten passcode or backup passphrase.

Backups

JPC can export an encrypted backup file. This is something you choose to do, not something that happens automatically.

  • The backup is sealed with a passphrase that you choose. We do not store that passphrase and we cannot recover it. If it is lost, the backup cannot be opened by anyone, including us.
  • Once the file is created, you decide where it goes. You may save it to your device, hand it to another app through the Android share sheet, or place it in a cloud service of your choosing.
  • If you choose to store a backup in a third-party service such as a cloud drive or an email account, that copy is then subject to that provider's terms and privacy practices, not this policy. We are not involved in that transfer and never receive the file.

Camera and card scanning

JPC can use your camera to scan insurance and identification cards so their details can be filled in automatically. The image is processed on your device, using text recognition that runs locally. Scanned images and the text read from them are stored on your device the same way as the rest of your records. No image and no recognized text is uploaded anywhere.

Permissions the app asks for, and why

  • Notifications so reminders and alarms can appear.
  • Alarms and reminders (exact alarms) so an appointment or medication alarm goes off at the time you set, rather than whenever the system finds it convenient.
  • Full-screen notifications so an alarm can take over the lock screen instead of arriving as a banner that is easy to miss.
  • Battery optimization exemption so the operating system does not put the app to sleep and silently skip your alarms. This is optional and the app will still run without it, but alarms become unreliable.
  • Camera only when you choose to scan a card or take a photo for a record.
  • Run at startup so alarms you already scheduled survive a device restart.
  • Vibration and wake lock so an alarm can wake the screen and be felt as well as heard.

JPC does not request location, contacts, microphone, or network permissions for the purpose of sending your data anywhere.

Children

JPC is intended for adults managing care, whether for themselves or for someone else. It is not directed at children and we do not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age, because we do not collect information at all.

Sharing and selling

We do not sell, rent, share, or disclose your information, because we never receive it. There is no third party with access to what you record in JPC.

If a legal authority were to demand your records from us, we would have nothing to produce.

Your control over your information

You can delete any record inside the app at any time. Uninstalling JPC removes the app's data from your device, including the encryption key. This is permanent, and it cannot be undone by reinstalling. If you intend to move to a new device or reinstall the app, export an encrypted backup first.

What JPC is not

JPC is a personal record-keeping tool. It is not a medical device, it does not provide medical advice, and it is not a substitute for professional care. Nothing in the app should be used to make a treatment decision on its own.

JPC is not a covered entity or a business associate under HIPAA, and this policy makes no claim of HIPAA compliance. HIPAA governs healthcare providers, health plans, and their business associates. JPC is a private notebook that belongs to you and stays on your device.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date at the top. If a future version of the app ever introduces a feature that transmits data, that change will be described here before the feature ships, and it will require your explicit choice to turn on.
